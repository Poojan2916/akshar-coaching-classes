and provide a secure location for government operations. Its history highlights the Maratha commitment

Case Study 2: The ?dny?patra and Foreign Traders

The ?dny?patra, written by Ramachandrapant Amatya in 1715, provides a rare insight into the Maratha
perspective on foreign influence. The text explicitly warns against the presence of European powers like
the Portuguese, English, Dutch, and French, referring to them as "hat-wearers." Amatya highlights that
these individuals were not mere traders but were fully intent on "increasing their territory" and
"propagating their religion." He stresses that their trade should be strictly controlled and that they should
not be allowed to construct strong, fortified buildings, as these would later serve as bases for colonial
encroachment. This document remains a primary source demonstrating the Maratha government’s
strategic awareness of early colonialism.
