Short Answer 1

What is Universal Adult Franchise? What is its constitutional basis?

Universal Adult Franchise = every citizen aged 18+ has the right to vote regardless of caste, religion, gender, education, or wealth. Constitutional basis: Article 326. Voting age was lowered from 21 to 18 by the 61st Constitutional Amendment, 1988. Principle: One Person = One Vote = One Value.
Short Answer 2

What is Public Interest Litigation? Why is it called the 'poor man's court'?

PIL allows any citizen or organization to approach the Supreme Court (Article 32) or High Court (Article 226) on behalf of those whose rights are violated, even without being a direct victim. A simple letter or telegram suffices. Called 'poor man's court' because it requires no complex procedures or high fees — accessible to all regardless of economic status.
Short Answer 3

What is the Chipko Movement? What was its impact?

Chipko Movement (1973, Uttarakhand) — villagers, especially women led by Gaura Devi, hugged trees to prevent commercial logging. 'Chipko' = to cling/hug. Impact: (1) Saved large areas of forest. (2) Highlighted link between forests and community livelihood. (3) Inspired environmental activism across India. (4) Led to a moratorium on felling in Himalayan forests.
Short Answer 4

Distinguish between a National Park and a Wildlife Sanctuary.

National Park: Strictly protected — no human activity whatsoever is allowed. Highest level of legal protection. Example: Kaziranga (Assam), Jim Corbett. Wildlife Sanctuary: Protects specific species — some limited human activity (like collection of forest produce) is permitted. Example: Bharatpur Bird Sanctuary. Both are protected areas under the Wildlife Protection Act, 1972.
