CASE STUDY 2: The Resource Economics of Portable Cells
Modern consumer electronics rely heavily on portable power storage. Single-use dry cells feature a
negative zinc container and a central carbon rod wrapped in a thick, moist paste-like electrolyte.
While inexpensive, they are discarded after their internal chemicals are exhausted. For high-drain
devices like smartphones and laptops, manufacturing industries have shifted to lithium-ion
technologies that utilize scarce global elements like lithium and cobalt. Because these materials are
highly concentrated in limited geographic zones, international focus has shifted toward dedicated
e-waste processing and solid-state alternatives.
