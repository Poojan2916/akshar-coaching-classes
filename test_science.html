Short Answer 1

What is Archimedes' Principle? Give one application.

Archimedes' Principle states: The buoyant force (upthrust) on an object immersed in a fluid equals the weight of the fluid displaced by the object. Application: Ships are designed with hollow shapes to displace large volumes of water, making the upthrust ≥ ship's weight so it floats.
Short Answer 2

Distinguish between boiling and evaporation.

Boiling: Occurs throughout the liquid; only at the fixed boiling point (100°C for water); rapid. Evaporation: Occurs only at the liquid surface; can happen at any temperature; slow. Both result in liquid→gas change.
Short Answer 3

Name any 3 organelles of a plant cell not found in an animal cell and give their functions.

(i) Cell wall — gives rigidity and structural support (made of cellulose). (ii) Chloroplast — site of photosynthesis; traps sunlight to make food. (iii) Large central vacuole — stores water, nutrients, waste; provides turgor pressure.
Short Answer 4

Explain the nitrogen cycle briefly.

Steps: (1) N₂ fixation — Rhizobium converts atmospheric N₂ into nitrates in legume root nodules. (2) Absorption — plants absorb nitrates to make proteins. (3) Consumption — animals eat plants. (4) Decomposition — dead matter → ammonia by decomposers. (5) Nitrification — ammonia → nitrates. (6) Denitrification — nitrates → N₂ back to atmosphere.
Short Answer 5

What are the factors that affect the strength of an electromagnet?

An electromagnet is stronger when: (1) Number of turns in the coil is increased — more turns = stronger field. (2) Current through the coil is increased — more current = stronger field. (3) Iron core is used instead of air — iron increases magnetic permeability. (4) Using soft iron core (easier to magnetize and demagnetize).
