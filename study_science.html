🌊 Buoyancy / Upthrust — Archimedes' Principle
📌 Archimedes' Principle: The upthrust (buoyant force) = Weight of fluid displaced by the object.

• Object floats → Upthrust ≥ Weight of object
• Object sinks → Weight > Upthrust
• Density < water → floats | Density > water → sinks
🚢 Ship floats because its hollow shape displaces a large volume of water → large upthrust ≥ ship's weight.
🎈 Helium balloon rises because upthrust from air > weight of balloon.
⚡ Electroscope — Detecting Electric Charge
• Electroscope = device to detect presence of electric charge
• Works on principle: Like charges repel → leaves spread apart
• Static electricity = electric charge at rest (generated by friction — rubbing)
• Electrostatic force can attract OR repel (unlike gravity which only attracts)

📌 Latent heat: Energy absorbed/released during phase change WITHOUT temperature changing. That's why the temperature-time graph has FLAT LINES at 0°C and 100°C.
📌 Boiling vs Evaporation: Boiling = throughout bulk, at fixed temp. Evaporation = surface only, at any temperature.

🌱 Nitrogen Cycle — Key Steps
1. N₂ fixation: Rhizobium (root nodules of legumes) + lightning → converts N₂ → nitrates
2. Absorption: Plants take nitrates → proteins → consumed by animals
3. Decomposition: Dead matter → ammonia (by decomposers)
4. Nitrification: Ammonia → nitrates (by nitrifying bacteria)
5. Denitrification: Nitrates → N₂ gas returned to atmosphere
⚠️ Nitrogen is essential for: Proteins, DNA, Chlorophyll, Vitamins — all living things need it!

⚡ Chapter Summary — Electricity: Magnetic & Heating Effects
🔑 Oersted (1820): Current-carrying wire creates a magnetic field around it.
When current stops → magnetic field disappears.
ConceptKey Facts
ElectromagnetCurrent-carrying coil + iron core = magnet that can be switched ON/OFF. Poles can be reversed by reversing current direction.
Strengthening electromagnetIncrease number of coil turns OR increase current magnitude OR use iron core.
Earth's magnetic fieldGenerated by movement of liquid iron in Earth's core. Helps birds/animals navigate. Shields Earth from harmful space particles.
ResistanceInternal opposition to current flow. Converts electrical energy → heat. Nichrome has higher resistance than copper.
Heating effectDepends on: material, thickness, length of wire, and magnitude of current. Used in heaters, irons, electric furnaces.
Dry cellZinc container = negative terminal. Carbon rod + metal cap = positive terminal. Electrolyte = thick moist paste.
Rechargeable batteriesLi-ion batteries: use lithium + cobalt. Can be charged and reused many times.
Solid-state batteriesReplace liquid/paste electrolyte with solid material. Safer, faster charging, longer life. Future technology.

⚠️ E-waste: Dead batteries contain lead, cadmium, lithium — toxic. Must be disposed at special e-waste recycling facilities, NOT regular garbage.
💡 Exam Tips — Electricity
🎯 Trick 1: "Oersted 1820" — if the question asks who discovered magnetic effect of current, it's always Hans Christian Oersted.
🎯 Trick 2: Dry cell = "dry" because electrolyte is thick PASTE not liquid. Don't say "no moisture" — it has some moisture.
🎯 Trick 3: In a dry cell — Zinc = Negative (Z = Zero = negative); Carbon + metal cap = Positive.
