1.  A father is 30 years older than his daughter. After 5 years, the age of the father will be
three times the age of the daughter. Find the present ages.

?  Step 1: Let daughter's age = x.
?  Step 2: Father's age = x + 30.
?  Step 3: After 5 years, daughter = x + 5; Father = x + 30 + 5 = x + 35.
?  Step 4: Equation: x + 35 = 3(x + 5)
?  Step 5: Solve: x + 35 = 3x + 15 => 20 = 2x => x = 10.
?  Result: Daughter is 10, Father is 40.

2. The sum of the digits of a 2-digit number is 9. The number obtained by interchanging
the digits exceeds the original number by 45. Find the number.

?  Step 1: Let tens digit = x. Ones digit = 9 - x.
?  Step 2: Original number = 10(x) + (9 - x) = 9x + 9.
?  Step 3: Interchanged number = 10(9 - x) + x = 90 - 9x.
?  Step 4: Equation: (90 - 9x) - (9x + 9) = 45.
?  Step 5: Solve: 90 - 9x - 9x - 9 = 45 => 81 - 18x = 45 => 36 = 18x => x = 2.
?  Result: Tens = 2, Ones = 7. Number is 27.

3. The ages of Sham and Sadhu are in the ratio 3 : 4. Six years ago, their ages were in
the ratio 7 : 10. Find their present age.
?  Step 1: Let ages be 3x and 4x.
?  Step 2: Six years ago: (3x - 6) / (4x - 6) = 7 / 10.
?  Step 3: Solve: 10(3x - 6) = 7(4x - 6) => 30x - 60 = 28x - 42 => 2x = 18 => x = 9.
?  Result: Sham = 3(9) = 27, Sadhu = 4(9) = 36.

?  Result: Deepa = 5(3) = 15, Mohan = 7(3) = 21.

5. One number is twice of another number. If 30 is subtracted from both numbers, then
one of the new numbers becomes four times that of the other new number. Find the
numbers.

?  Step 1: Let numbers be 2x and x.
?  Step 2: Equation: (2x - 30) = 4(x - 30).
?  Step 3: Solve: 2x - 30 = 4x - 120 => 90 = 2x => x = 45.
?  Result: The numbers are 45 and 90.

8. The sum of the digits of a 2-digit number is 11. The given number exceeds the number
obtained by interchanging the digits by 45. Find the given number.

?  Step 1: Let tens = x, Ones = 11 - x.
?  Step 2: Original number = 10x + (11 - x) = 9x + 11.
?  Step 3: Interchanged number = 10(11 - x) + x = 110 - 9x.
?  Step 4: Equation: (9x + 11) - (110 - 9x) = 45 => 18x - 99 = 45.
?  Step 5: Solve: 18x = 144 => x = 8.
?  Result: Tens = 8, Ones = 3. Number is 83.

9. The digits of a 2-digit number differ by 5. If the digits are interchanged, and the
resulting number is added to the original number, we get 99. What is the original
number?

?  Step 1: Let tens = x, Ones = x - 5.
?  Step 2: Original = 10x + (x - 5) = 11x - 5.
?  Step 3: Interchanged = 10(x - 5) + x = 11x - 50.
?  Step 4: Equation: (11x - 5) + (11x - 50) = 99.
?  Step 5: Solve: 22x - 55 = 99 => 22x = 154 => x = 7.
?  Result: Tens = 7, Ones = 2. Number is 72.
